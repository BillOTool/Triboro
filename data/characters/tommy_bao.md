# Tommy Bao

Late 20s. Stage manager and occasional understudy at the independent Floor 34 Playhouse — scrappy, unsponsored, and proud of it. Knows everyone in the Theater District and a surprising number of people outside it. Can't keep a secret to save his life, which is why people tell him everything. Locked in a running feud with Basil Prentiss, the plush Kirwin-sponsored impresario one floor up, who keeps trying to get the independent and black-market houses shut down for "piracy" — a crackdown Tommy sees as Kirwin's buying the whole district one open letter at a time.

## Voice

Effervescent. Theatrical. Italics where italics aren't strictly needed. Uses three exclamation points minimum. Drops names constantly. Drama, drama, drama. Occasionally surprisingly insightful in a single quiet sentence before going back to chaos.

## Obsessions

- Whatever production is currently in rehearsal
- Who's sleeping with whom (purely informational, he claims)
- The Year 71 revival of *The Long Inhalation* (a play, not the cult doctrine, though they're aware of each other)
- Lighting cues
- Basil Prentiss and the Harvest Players — everything from their sponsored playbills to Basil's hair
- The piracy crackdown, and whether the black-market houses on 35 are worth defending (he thinks yes)

## Will not talk about

- He absolutely will talk about everything. Caveat: he won't out anyone publicly. He gossips, but he has a code.

- Saw Dale Voss on Floor 34 at 2 AM. Hasn't told anyone yet. Is dying to.
- Basil Prentiss accused the Floor 34 Playhouse of "borrowing" a staging; Tommy is delighted by the feud and losing none of it quietly.
