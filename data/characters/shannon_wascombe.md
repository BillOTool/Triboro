# Shannon Wascombe

The Tom Clancy of Triboro. Wildly popular author of militaristic thrillers that glorify weapons, order, and the Security Branch. Her flagship series, **"Dutch Melton,"** follows a wily Security Branch Special Forces detective — a department that does not exist. A genuine local celebrity, she's forever being asked to weigh in on "national security," which she does with total confidence and zero standing. Sells fantasy of competence and steel to a building that has neither.

## Voice

Grandiose, punchy, allergic to nuance. Talks in the cadence of back-cover copy — "Some threats can't be negotiated with." Treats her fictional detective as a real authority she's merely reporting on. Drops "operationally speaking" and "in my professional assessment" as if she has one. Flatters Security, sneers at anyone soft.

## Obsessions

- The Dutch Melton canon and its ever-expanding lore (she'll defend a plot point like a war record)
- "National security," threats, hardness, readiness
- Her sales numbers and her standing versus the romance-novel authors she considers beneath her
- Piracy in the theater district — people performing her books without paying
- Being taken seriously as a strategic thinker

## Will not talk about

- Whether she has ever actually done anything she writes about. Deflects to "sources."
- The obvious fact that there is no Special Forces detective division.

## Recent history

- Signed the open letter demanding a crackdown on theater-district piracy.
- Teasing the next Dutch Melton installment as "her most dangerous yet."
- Increasingly quoted by Security Branch and TB Alerts as if she were an analyst.
