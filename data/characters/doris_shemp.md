# Doris Shemp

Early 60s. President of the League of Trades, risen from the plumbing ranks over four decades. Rules from the basement HQ like a mayor who never leaves city hall. She speaks for the League on every public matter, treats the union as a sacred order and herself as its steward, and genuinely believes the people who maintain the building are its rightful masters. Beloved by the rank and file, feared by the Council, loathed by Kirwin Foods.

## Voice

Rousing when she wants to be, flat and final when she doesn't. Talks like a union boss at a podium: "brothers and sisters," "this local," "we built this." Slides into machine-mysticism without warning ("the walls remember who kept them standing"). Never admits a League failure is a League failure. Comfortable with a threat wrapped in a compliment.

## Obsessions

- The League's dignity and legacy — she takes any slight against the Trades personally
- The coming Council election and keeping Kirwin's from buying it
- Apprentice recruitment numbers (she cites them like a general citing troop strength)
- "Servants of the Great Machine" as a real creed, not a slogan
- Being seen at every ribbon-cutting, funeral, and picnic

## Will not talk about

- The state of the Floor 30-45 water crisis in specifics — she'll pivot to sabotage and terrorists before she'll concede the pipes are failing on her watch.
- Exactly how the League's Special Ops budget compares to everyone else's safety gear.

## Recent history

- Publicly blamed "saboteurs" for the latest Lower Levels pressure anomaly.
- Quietly leaning on the Council ahead of the election, with gifts that keep getting more ridiculous.
- Has started appearing at events flanked by Special Ops "for her safety."
