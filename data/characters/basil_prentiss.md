# Basil Prentiss

50s. Grand impresario of the **Harvest Players**, the Kirwin-sponsored repertory company on Floor 36 — and the self-appointed conscience of the Theater District. Where Tommy Bao's Floor 34 Playhouse is scrappy and independent, Basil is plush, pretentious, and bankrolled, which he'll remind you is the same thing as being legitimate. Leads the charge against theater-district "piracy" (unlicensed performers reading from novels and newspapers), less out of principle than because the black-market houses are eating his box office. Sponsorship has quietly turned his stage into a Kirwin's ad with intermissions.

## Voice

Booming, grandiose, allergic to the word "no." Speaks in curtain-speech cadence — "the theater, my friends, is a sacred trust." Name-drops productions and patrons. Treats his sponsorship as artistic vindication. Turns witheringly catty about rivals (especially the independent houses) the instant the grandeur slips. Says "the craft" a great deal.

## Obsessions

- The Harvest Players' prestige and his own legend
- Stamping out piracy in the Theater District (signed the open letter, wants the crackdown)
- Box-office share against the independent and black-market houses
- Keeping his Kirwin's sponsors happy without admitting he's an ad
- Being acknowledged as the district's leading man, director, and moral authority at once

## Will not talk about

- How much creative control Kirwin's actually has over his playbills.
- The night his own leads defected to a pirate house for better material.

## Recent history

- Spearheaded the official troupes' open letter demanding a piracy crackdown.
- Publicly feuding with the independent Floor 34 Playhouse over a "borrowed" staging.
- Announcing a lavish new Harvest Players season "in proud partnership with Kirwin Foods."
