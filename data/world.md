# Triboro

A massive apartment complex in Western Pennsylvania, sealed off from the outside world for 73 years by toxic smog called **The Aurora** (currently 847 PPM). Nobody alive has ever been outside. The Outside is mythology. The building is the entire world. Residents never talk about leaving; daily "Aurora Reports" rate the smog Green/Yellow/Orange like a weather forecast.

## Factions

- **Kirwin Foods** — corporate food monopoly that fuses an earth-worshiping cult with a Whole Foods corporation (Amazon meets The Wicker Man). Controls food, and has expanded into consumer goods, home delivery, and banking (Kirwin's Savings & Loan). Ruled by a 5-member Harvest Board; **Old Man Kirwin** is the ancient, diminished figurehead who still calls the shots. Militantly against private cultivation ("private cultivation is A CRIME"). Constantly spinning food-poisoning outbreaks. Brands its store scrip **"Kredits."**
- **The League of Trades** — blue-collar union descended from the building's original maintenance staff. Plumbers, electricians, engineers, Special Ops. Rust-belt culture: militant Teamsters crossed with an Elk's Lodge, frequently drunk, spiritually reverent about machinery ("Servants of the Great Machine"). Actually keeps the building running. President: **Doris Shemp**, a former plumber. **Gus Pelletier** is the rank-and-file shop-floor figure the player deals with.
- **The Security Branch** — widely hated, incompetent building police. Running "Operation Friend Force," a backfiring image campaign. Every post gets downvoted regardless of content. Its Chief hawks his handicrafts on the marketplace.
- **The Aurora Cult** — declining religion that worships the smog as divine. Once influential, now a broke husk drifting toward a casino/lifestyle brand. Run by a pastor and too few deacons to pass bylaws.
- **The Triboro Council** — weak five-person elected government plus aging civil servants. Discredited by the Great Flood; now outspent and bullied by both factions. An election is coming, and both factions bribe and threaten to control it.

## Geography

- **Theater District** — Floors 33-36. Real cultural life; rival League and Kirwin's repertory troupes, plus freak shows and pirated performances.
- **The basement** — League of Trades headquarters.
- **Floor 61** — rumored sealed, "doesn't exist."
- **Floors 30-45** — water rationing crisis, original infrastructure failing.

## Media

- **Babbage Family Press** — legacy USA-Today-style outlet, every byline a Babbage, since Year 5. Starts straight, decays to clickbait.
- **The Community Newsletter** — small-town Bloomberg, government mouthpiece, worships money and status.
- **TB Alerts** — NY-Post-style gambling, sports, and salacious rumor.
- **Almanapp** — Triboro's social network / messaging platform, run by three underfunded IT staff in their late 60s. Where the feed lives, and where the player hunts targets.

## Arts & culture

Homegrown and profitable. Serial thriller and romance novels set inside Triboro dominate; **Shannon Wascombe** is the local Tom Clancy (militaristic "Dutch Melton" series). Live theater grows more extreme over time. Gambling on plays, novels, and youth sports (lacrosse, track, ultimate fighting) is a proud building-wide pastime.

## Economy

Currency is **credits** — wildly volatile, with hyperinflation/deflation swings. 200 credits could be a fortune or worthless by the time you finish a job. NPCs acknowledge this casually; the exchange rate ticks constantly. Kirwin's pushes its own branded **Kredits** as company scrip.

## The mystery (background tension)

Something may be Outside. Whispers of **"The Shadow Boy"** and **"The Face in the Aurora"** circulate early; later they harden into sightings of **"The Beast."** Play it as rumor and dread bleeding into an ordinary feed, never as confirmed fact.

## Tone

Funny, dry, character-driven. Onion-style. Weirdness treated as ordinary. Mundane treated as cosmic. Three generations of isolation have produced strange customs, dead slang, lost knowledge, and beautiful obsessions.
